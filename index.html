Who we are

Over two decades turning activism into evidence.

Founded in 1999 and headquartered in Kampala, Raising Voices Uganda is a pioneer in violence prevention across East and Central Africa. Through the SASA! Together community mobilisation programme and the Good School Toolkit, we equip communities, schools and local organisations to prevent violence before it happens.

As our work scales toward 5,000 partner schools, the evidence behind it must scale too. This repository unifies incident tracking, referral coordination and the full research archive into one role-based system — so every report strengthens the evidence base without ever exposing the people behind it.

Case management workflow

From first disclosure to a closed, accountable case

Every GBV report follows the same structured, auditable path through the system — no report is lost, no step happens off the record, and every action is attributed to a named staff member.

S1

Disclosure received

A field activist or case officer receives the report.

The intake form captures incident type, location, approximate date, survivor consent preferences and immediate safety concerns. Every field is mandatory before submission, preventing incomplete records from entering the system.

S2

Safety & needs screening

Immediate risk assessed before any referral is made.

A structured risk-screening tool flags immediate safety concerns. Cases meeting any of the six escalation criteria are automatically elevated to a senior officer before proceeding to the next step.

S3

Case officer assigned

A named officer takes full ownership of the case.

Assignment is logged with a timestamp and the officer’s staff ID. The case appears on their active queue immediately, and they are responsible for all subsequent actions until formal closure or escalation.

S4

Referral actioned

Medical, legal or psychosocial referrals sent to partners.

Referrals are dispatched directly to verified Ugandan partner agencies from within the system. Each carries a unique tracking ID linked back to the originating case, so completion can be confirmed and recorded.

S5

Follow-up & review

Outcomes verified against the original needs assessment.

Scheduled follow-up prompts ensure the assigned officer confirms that each referral was received, acted upon and that the survivor’s immediate needs have been met. Any unresolved needs trigger a new referral cycle.

S6

Case closed

Archived with a complete, immutable audit trail.

Closure requires a mandatory summary note from the assigned officer. The case is then anonymised and archived. The full audit trail is locked as read-only, satisfying Uganda Data Protection & Privacy Act, 2019 obligations.
